Academic Research and Key Projects

My academic research at Wuhan University of Technology focuses on applying embedded systems and artificial intelligence to solve practical navigation challenges.

Design of a Blind Path Guiding System Based on Semantic Segmentation

Deep Learning Semantic Segmentation Image Processing NVIDIA TensorRT

Description: In my graduation project, I created a guiding device to help visually impaired individuals navigate safely. The system uses a lightweight semantic segmentation network to identify passable tactile paving and potential obstacles from camera input, providing voice instructions to users.

Key Achievements:

  • Ported a lightweight neural network to an intelligent embedded system.
  • Utilized NVIDIA TensorRT high-performance engine for model inference acceleration, enabling real-time processing.
  • Proposed an image-processing-based obstacle avoidance algorithm to enhance navigation safety.

Research on BeiDou-Based Swift Deployment System for Miniature AGVs

BDS/GPS 3D Environment Construction Autonomous AGV Trajectory Planning

Description: This research project involves developing a system for rapidly deploying automated guided vehicles (AGVs) in outdoor environments. We integrated BeiDou (BDS) and GPS signals to achieve precise positioning, enabling collision-free trajectory planning.

Key Achievements:

  • Used compound-eye cameras to collect environmental data and construct 3D visualized scenes.
  • The system supports quick deployment of miniature AGVs to target locations beyond the visible range.
  • Patent: This work led to a registered software copyright: "BeiDou-Based Dual-Mode AGV Positioning System" (Registration No: 2023SR0232320)
